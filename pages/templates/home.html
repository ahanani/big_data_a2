Bike Purchase Predictive Model



Introducing a predictive model that uses a range of demographic and socioeconomic features to predict whether a customer is likely to purchase a bike. The model includes six key variables - gender, income, education, occupation, commute distance, and age - and is based on a RandomForestClassifier algorithm. The model's performance is measured using RMSE (root mean squared error), with an average score of 0.64.

Through the utilization of this predictive model, we can accurately determine whether a customer is potentially inclined to purchase a bike. In cases where the model indicates that a customer is likely to make a purchase, we plan to offer them a lucrative discount of 20% on regular bike prices, thereby optimizing our marketing and sales strategy.

On the other hand, if the model indicates that a customer is unlikely to make a purchase at present, we will inform them that they do not qualify for any current discounts. This strategy allows us to capitalize on potential bike purchasers while minimizing the likelihood of offering discounts to those less likely to make a purchase, ultimately enhancing the overall efficiency and profitability of our business operations.
